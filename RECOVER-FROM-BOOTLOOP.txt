================================================================================
RECOVER FROM BOOT LOOP (black screen, vibrating)
================================================================================

STEP 1: GET TO FASTBOOT MODE (do this first)
---------------------------------------------
The tablet is looping before Android starts. You need to force it into
bootloader (fastboot) mode so we can fix it from the PC.

1. Let the tablet run until the battery dies, OR hold POWER for 15+ seconds
   until it turns off (might take several long presses).

2. With the tablet OFF, connect it to the PC with USB (optional but helpful).

3. Enter fastboot. Try these in order (hold for 10–15 seconds until screen
   changes or stays black but PC may detect fastboot):
   - POWER + VOLUME DOWN
   - POWER + VOLUME UP
   - POWER + BOTH VOLUME buttons
   - VOLUME DOWN only (then press Power briefly)

4. When you see FASTBOOT or "Download mode" or the screen stays black:
   On the PC run:  fastboot devices
   If you see a device ID, the tablet is in fastboot. Go to STEP 2.

5. If nothing works, try RECOVERY instead:
   Power off, then hold POWER + VOLUME UP until you see recovery menu.
   In recovery: "Wipe data / factory reset" then "Reboot". That may fix the loop.

STEP 2: FIX FROM PC (tablet in fastboot)
----------------------------------------
Open PowerShell in the folder with fastboot (or use full path). Run:

  fastboot devices

If you see your device, run the recovery script:

  C:\Users\jrrou\tablet-gsi\UNBRICK-FASTBOOT.ps1

That script will:
  - Wipe data (fixes most boot loops)
  - Optionally reflash the GSI system image if you still have system.img
  - Reboot

Or do it manually:
  fastboot -w
  fastboot reboot

If it still loops after -w, reflash the GSI (you need system.img in tablet-gsi):
  fastboot flash system_a C:\Users\jrrou\tablet-gsi\system.img
  fastboot -w
  fastboot reboot

STEP 3: WHEN POWER + VOLUME DOES NOTHING
-----------------------------------------
The tablet may never be fully "off" while looping, so the keys don't work.
Do this:

A. LET THE BATTERY DIE (most important)
   - Stop charging. Let it boot-loop until the battery is completely dead
     (screen stays black, no more vibrations). Can take 1–3 hours.
   - Do not press any buttons during this time.
   - Once it’s fully dead, wait 10–15 minutes.

B. TRY BUTTONS AGAIN FROM COLD
   - (Optional) Plug in USB to the PC, but don’t charge yet or only briefly.
   - Hold POWER + VOLUME DOWN and keep holding for 20–30 seconds.
   - If the screen stays black, try: plug in the charger while still holding
     POWER + VOL DOWN for another 15 seconds.
   - Try POWER + VOLUME UP for 20–30 seconds.
   - Try VOLUME DOWN only, hold 5 sec, then press and hold POWER for 15 sec.
   - On some Lenovo tablets you must release POWER as soon as the logo
     appears, then press VOL DOWN repeatedly to get “Fastboot”.

C. LENOVO RESCUE (Software Fix) – no buttons needed if PC sees the device
   - Download: Lenovo “Rescue and Smart Assistant” (or “Software Fix”)
     from support.lenovo.com (search “Rescue and Smart Assistant” or
     “Software Fix Lenovo”).
   - Install on your Windows PC. Open the tool.
   - Connect the tablet to the PC with USB while it is looping (or after
     battery died and you plugged it in).
   - The tool may detect the device when it briefly passes through a
     connectable state during the loop (or when it powers on from dead).
   - If it detects the tablet, follow the wizard to “Rescue” / flash stock
     firmware. That will wipe data and install stock Android (you can
     re-flash the GSI later if you want).
   - If it never detects: keep the tablet plugged in, try the button combos
     again after a full battery drain (step A).

D. IF STILL NOTHING
   - Charge the tablet (off or looping) for 2+ hours so the battery isn’t
     low. Then repeat step B from a full power-off (let it die again if
     needed).
   - Search: “Lenovo [your model number] enter fastboot” or “bootloader
     mode” for your exact device.
   - As a last resort: Lenovo support or a repair shop can try EDL/blank
     flash if the hardware is still good.
